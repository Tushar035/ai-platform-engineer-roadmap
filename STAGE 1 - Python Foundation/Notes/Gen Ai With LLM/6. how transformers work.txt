The transcript starts with a very important point:

> Building LLMs using transformers dramatically improved performance compared to RNNs




---

🔥 What does this actually mean?

Before transformers:

Models struggled with:

Context

Long sentences

Accuracy



After transformers:

Huge improvements in:

Understanding language

Generating text

Handling complex tasks



👉 This is why we saw an explosion in generative AI


---

🧩 2. The Core Power of Transformers


---

🧠 Key Idea (VERY IMPORTANT)

> Transformers understand relationships between ALL words in a sentence




---

❌ Old thinking (RNN)

Word only looks at:

Previous words




---

✅ Transformer thinking

Each word looks at:

Every other word in the sentence




---

🔄 Visual Intuition

Sentence: The teacher taught the student with the book

teacher ↔ student ↔ book ↔ taught ↔ with
   ↘        ↓        ↙
   All words connected


---

💡 Why this matters

This allows the model to answer:

Who has the book?

Who is doing the action?

Is the book important?


👉 This is true language understanding


---

🧠 3. Attention Weights (How Model Understands Importance)


---

📌 What are Attention Weights?

Numbers that represent:

How important one word is to another




---

🧾 Example

Sentence:

> "The teacher taught the student with the book"



The model learns:

"book" → strongly related to:

"teacher"

"student"




---

📊 Attention Map

A visual representation of:

Word-to-word importance




---

🔄 Flow

Each Word Pair
     ↓
Calculate Relationship Strength
     ↓
Assign Attention Weight
     ↓
Build Context Understanding


---

🧠 Important Insight

> These weights are NOT manually set
They are learned during training




---

🧠 4. Self-Attention (Core Mechanism)


---

📌 Definition

Each word attends to:

All other words in the same sentence




---

💡 Why “self”?

Because attention happens:

Within the same input




---

🎯 Result

Model captures:

Meaning

Relationships

Context




---

---

🏗️ Two Main Components

1. Encoder

Understands input text


2. Decoder

Generates output text



---

🔄 Flow

Input Text
     ↓
Encoder (understanding)
     ↓
Decoder (generation)
     ↓
Output Text

📌 Important Note from Transcript

Diagram convention:

Input → bottom

Output → top

🧠 6. Machines Don't Understand Words — Only Numbers


---

📌 Key Concept

> Machine learning models are statistical calculators



They work with:

Numbers ❗

Not words



---

🔢 7. Tokenization (Converting Words → Numbers)


---

📌 What happens?

Words are converted into:

Token IDs (numbers)




---

🧾 Example

Sentence:

> "I love AI"



Tokenized:

I → 101

love → 205

AI → 999



---

🧠 Important Detail from Transcript

Different tokenization methods:

Whole words

Parts of words (subwords)



---

🔄 Flow

Text Input
     ↓
Tokenizer
     ↓
Token IDs


---

⚠️ Critical Rule

> Same tokenizer must be used during:



Training ✅

Inference ✅



---

🧠 8. Embedding Layer (Numbers → Meaning)


---

📌 What is Embedding?

Converts tokens into:

Vectors (arrays of numbers)




---

🧠 What is a Vector?

Example:

"AI" → [0.21, 0.78, 0.11, …]



---

🌌 High-Dimensional Space

Each word:

Placed in a multi-dimensional space




---

🧾 From Transcript

Original transformer used:

Vector size = 512 dimensions




---

🔄 Flow

Token IDs
     ↓
Embedding Layer
     ↓
Vector Representation


---

💡 Intuition

Words with similar meaning:

Close in space




---

🧾 Example

"king" near "queen"

"apple" far from "car"



---

🧠 Mathematical Understanding

Distance between words:

Calculated using angles



👉 This allows model to:

Understand relationships mathematically



---

🧠 9. Positional Encoding (Order Matters!)


---

❗ Problem

Transformers process words:

In parallel


👉 So they lose:

Word order



---

✅ Solution

Add positional encoding


---

🔄 Flow

Token Vectors
     ↓
Add Position Information
     ↓
Position-Aware Vectors


---

🧾 Example

Sentence:

> "Dog bites man" vs "Man bites dog"



👉 Same words, different meaning


---

💡 Positional encoding preserves:

Word order

Sentence meaning



---

🧠 10. Self-Attention Layer (Core Processing)


---

📌 What happens here?

Model analyzes:

Relationships between tokens




---

🔄 Flow

Input Vectors
     ↓
Compare Tokens
     ↓
Apply Attention Weights
     ↓
Contextual Output


---

🧠 Output

Each word now:

Contains information about:

Other words




---

🧠 11. Multi-Head Attention (Parallel Understanding)


---

📌 What is it?

Multiple attention mechanisms run in parallel



---

📊 From Transcript

Number of heads:

Usually 12 to 100




---

🧠 Why multiple heads?

Each head learns:

Different aspects of language



---

🧾 Examples from Transcript

One head → people (teacher, student)

One head → activity (taught)

One head → other patterns (like rhyme)



---

🔄 Flow

Input
 ↓ ↓ ↓ ↓
Head1 Head2 Head3 HeadN
 ↓ ↓ ↓ ↓
Combine Results


---

⚠️ Important Insight

You DO NOT define:

What each head learns



👉 Model learns automatically


---

🧠 Additional Insight

Some attention maps:

Easy to interpret


Others:

Very complex




---

🧠 12. Feedforward Network


---

📌 What happens?

Processes attention output

Produces logits



---

📌 What are logits?

Raw scores for:

Each word in vocabulary




---

🔄 Flow

Attention Output
      ↓
Feedforward Network
      ↓
Logits


---

🧠 13. Softmax Layer (Final Decision)


---

📌 What happens?

Converts logits → probabilities



---

🧾 Example

Word	Score

cat	0.7
dog	0.2
car	0.1



---

🔄 Flow

Logits
   ↓
Softmax
   ↓
Probability Distribution


---

📌 Important Detail

Output includes:

Probability for EVERY word in vocabulary


Vocabulary size:

Thousands of words




---

🎯 Final Selection

Choose:

Highest probability word

OR sample (for creativity)




---
